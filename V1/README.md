# FarmTrack V1

FarmTrack V1 was the first prototype of the Farm-Track project.

The objective was to design and build a manually controlled off-road robot capable of picking and transporting small objects during a robotics competition. This version established the basic mechanical layout and served as the foundation for all future iterations.

---

## Features

- Four-wheel differential drive
- Wired control
- Articulated arm
- Parallel jaw gripper
- Steel chassis
- 12 V DC geared motors

## Design Overview

The drivetrain used four independently driven wheels in a differential drive configuration.

An articulated arm with a parallel jaw gripper allowed the bot to pick and place objects during the competition.

The control system was completely wired, which kept the electronics simple but limited the operating range and maneuverability.

---

## Limitations

During testing and competition, several areas for improvement became apparent:

- Limited drivetrain torque on rough terrain
- High motor weight at the end of the arm
- Poor weight distribution
- Limited driving control
- Wired operation reduced mobility

These observations became the design goals for FarmTrack V2.
