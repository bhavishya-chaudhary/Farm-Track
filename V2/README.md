# FarmTrack V2

FarmTrack V2 was developed by improving the original FarmTrack V1 instead of designing a completely new robot.

The primary focus was to address the mechanical limitations identified during testing and competition. Most of the improvements were aimed at increasing drivetrain torque, reducing the load on the arm, and improving the overall mechanical design.

---

## Features

- Higher torque drivetrain
- Increased gearbox reduction
- Worm-and-wheel arm mechanism
- Flexible shaft gripper drive
- Improved weight distribution
- Manual PWM speed control
- Improved wiring

## Design Improvements

### Drivetrain

The gearbox was modified to increase the overall reduction ratio.

Although the maximum speed decreased, the additional torque significantly improved the robot's performance on rough terrain.

### Arm

The direct-drive mechanism was replaced with a worm-and-wheel drive.

This provided a self-locking mechanism that allowed the arm to hold its position without continuous motor power.

Rubber bands were also introduced to provide a simple counterbalancing force and reduce the load on the arm motor.

### Gripper

The gripper motor was relocated from the end of the arm to the chassis.

Motion was transmitted using a flexible shaft made from a modified speedometer cable.

This reduced the moving mass of the arm and improved weight distribution.

A worm gear mechanism allowed the gripper to maintain its grip without continuous motor power.

### Control System

Manual PWM speed control provided smoother operation compared to the previous version.

The wiring was also redesigned to improve current handling and simplify maintenance.

## Competition Result

## Lessons Learned

FarmTrack V2 was the first version where the project shifted from simply building a working robot to improving an existing design through engineering iteration.

The experience gained from this version directly influenced the development of FarmTrack V3.
